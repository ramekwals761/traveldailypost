**Operational Failure & Regulatory Breach**

On March 21, 2026, **Air India** deployed a Boeing 777-200LR for flight AI185 (New Delhi/IGI to Vancouver/YVR) without prior authorization from Transport Canada or approval from Canada's civil aviation authority. Approximately 18 minutes into the flight, the discrepancy was identified through real-time regulatory verification systems. The aircraft, registration number `VT-ALH` (Air India's 777-200LR), was immediately ordered to return to Indira Gandhi International Airport (IGI/DEL), costing the airline an 8.5-hour repositioning flight and stranding 324 passengers mid-route.

**Root Cause & Fleet Management Gap**

Air India's internal investigation revealed that a ground operations supervisor in Delhi confused flight AI185's assigned aircraft with an alternate Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner (the approved variant for Canadian routes). The 787-9, which operates DEL-YVR with Transport Canada approval under Air India's international air operator's certificate (IAOC), was replaced without updating the flight manifest or cross-checking with the regulatory compliance database. This represents a **critical failure in pre-departure verification**, where aircraft type, certification status, and route-specific approvals must be verified against a centralized checklist before departure.
