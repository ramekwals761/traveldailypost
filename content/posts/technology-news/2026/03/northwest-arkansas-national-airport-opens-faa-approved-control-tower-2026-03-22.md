## Full Coverage: What We Know

Northwest Arkansas National Airport officially commissioned its new Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)-approved air traffic control tower on **March 22, 2026**, following completion of a $87.5 million infrastructure project. The 203-foot facility replaces the airport's 40-year-old control tower and integrates cutting-edge technology including advanced Surface Detection Equipment (SDE), Terminal Radar Approach Control (TRACON) systems, and automated weather observation platforms. The tower is now managing all air traffic operations for the facility, which served approximately 3.2 million passengers in 2025.

The project began in 2023 as part of XNA's capital improvement plan to address rising passenger traffic and operational demands. Northwest Arkansas has experienced consistent year-over-year growth, with a 12% increase in passengers from 2024 to 2025. The aging control tower posed operational constraints and limited the airport's ability to accommodate larger aircraft and increased flight frequency. FAA officials identified the upgrade as critical infrastructure necessary to maintain safety standards and support regional economic development.

The Federal Aviation Administration granted final certification after six months of operational testing and validation. "This new facility represents a significant advancement in air traffic safety and operational efficiency for the region," said **FAA Regional Administrator Thomas Mitchell** during the activation ceremony. "The technology deployed here meets the highest safety standards and positions Northwest Arkansas for sustained growth over the next two decades."

The new tower directly impacts the 14 airlines currently operating from XNA, including Southwest Airlines, American Airlines, Delta Air Lines, United Airlines, and regional carriers. Enhanced operational capacity allows these carriers to expand service without creating ground delays or air traffic congestion. The facility's improved weather detection systems provide more accurate real-time data to pilots, reducing weather-related diversions and cancellations by an estimated 8-12%.

The FAA has scheduled a 90-day operational validation period, during which controllers will optimize procedures and refine coordination with neighboring facilities at Memphis International (MEM), Dallas-Fort Worth (DFW), and Tulsa International (TUL). Full commercial operational status without restrictions is expected by **June 22, 2026**.
