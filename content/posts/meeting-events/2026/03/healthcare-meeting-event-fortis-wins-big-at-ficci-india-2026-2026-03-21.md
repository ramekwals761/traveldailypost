## What This Means for Travelers

- **Book your procedure now:** Indian hospitals offering FICCI-recognized excellence are booking faster. Plan 4-6 months ahead for elective surgeries to lock in dates and coordinate travel logistics.

- **Verify accreditation status:** Confirm hospitals hold JCI (Joint Commission International) or NABH (National Accreditation Board for Hospitals) certification. Fortis's awards validate these credentials—ask for proof before committing.

- **Bundle travel with recovery time:** Medical tourism trips typically require 10-21 days on-site (3-5 for surgery, 7-14 for recovery). Budget flights, accommodation, and a companion's expenses into your total cost.

- **Check visa requirements early:** Medical visas (`M-visas`) for India require hospital letters and financial proof. Process applications 8-10 weeks before your surgery date—FICCI-recognized hospitals can expedite documentation.

- **Leverage medical tourism packages:** FICCI-connected networks now offer all-inclusive bundles (surgery, 3-5 nights hospital stay, airport transfers, follow-up consultations). These run 20-35% cheaper than booking piecemeal.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a healthcare meeting event like FICCI 2026 proof a hospital is safe for international patients?**
Partially. FICCI recognition indicates institutional credibility and commitment to standards, but it's not regulatory approval. Always cross-check with JCI, NABH, or your home country's health authority. Fortis's six awards suggest strong operational excellence—still, ask for specific complication rates, surgeon credentials, and infection control certifications before booking.

**How do I book a procedure at a FICCI award-winning hospital as an international patient?**
Contact the hospital's "International Patient Services" desk directly (not travel brokers). Request a formal quote including surgery, anesthesia, hospital stay, and post-op follow-ups. Provide your medical reports; the hospital will assign a coordinating doctor. Most FICCI-recognized hospitals like Fortis process medical visas and arrange accommodation within 3-4 weeks.

**Will my insurance cover surgery in India if the hospital won a FICCI award?**
Depends on your insurer and policy. Some US/UK insurers cover Indian procedures if the hospital holds JCI accreditation (which FICCI award-winners typically do). File pre-authorization 6-8 weeks before surgery. Medicare and NHS don't cover overseas procedures, but private insurers (United, Cigna, Bupa) often do at 70-80% reimbursement.
