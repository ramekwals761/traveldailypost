## What Happened: Context & Timeline

Travel and tourism operators across Southeast Asia and East Asia are reporting significant increases in bookings as the Hari Raya holiday approaches in 2026. **Japan and China** are topping the list for international arrivals, while Malaysia, Indonesia, and Singapore are seeing domestic and regional travel surges. Hari Raya (Eid al-Fitr) marks the end of Ramadan and traditionally sparks major travel movements in Muslim-majority nations and regions. This year's timing has aligned with spring break periods in several countries, creating a "perfect storm" of overlapping travel demand across Asia.

Destination marketing organizations report booking increases of 15-20% compared to the same period in 2025, with airfare and accommodation prices reflecting the heightened demand. Airlines have added extra flights to major hubs like Tokyo, Beijing, Kuala Lumpur, Jakarta, and Singapore to accommodate surging passenger numbers.

- **Expect crowded airports and transit:** Plan for longer security queues, immigration wait times, and potential flight delays during peak dates (March 28-April 5). Arrive 3-4 hours early for international flights.

## Industry Context & Analysis

The convergence of Hari Raya celebrations with spring travel season is creating record demand across Asia-Pacific travel corridors. Destination marketing organizations have noted that travelers from China and Japan are increasingly exploring Southeast Asian cities during this period, while residents of Malaysia, Indonesia, and Singapore are booking outbound trips to East Asia.

Airline capacity is stretched, with carriers like **ANA, China Southern, Malaysia Airlines, Garuda Indonesia, and Singapore Airlines** all reporting near-full aircraft on primary routes. This supply constraint is the primary driver of price increases. Regional low-cost carriers are also benefiting, with budget airlines reporting load factors above 85% on key routes.

Hotels and resorts are implementing dynamic pricing strategies, with beachfront properties in Bali, Langkawi, and Penang commanding premiums up to 25% above standard rates. However, mid-range and business hotels still offer reasonable value for budget-conscious travelers who book in the next 5-7 days.
