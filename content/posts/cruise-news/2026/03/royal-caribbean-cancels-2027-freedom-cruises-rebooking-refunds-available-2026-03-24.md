## What This Means for Travelers

- **Act immediately on your option:** Refund or rebooking deadlines are firm—typically 30–60 days from the cancellation notice. Contact Royal Caribbean directly or check your email for the exact deadline to avoid automatic refund processing.

- **Choose rebooking strategically:** If you opt to rebook, Royal Caribbean is offering flexible date and ship options. Consider rebooking on comparable ships (e.g., *Voyager of the Seas*, *Explorer of the Seas*) if your original dates don't suit your schedule.

Yes. Royal Caribbean is offering full monetary refunds to all affected passengers. Contact the cruise line directly through your booking confirmation or call Royal Caribbean customer service. Refunds are typically processed to the original payment method within 30–45 days of your request.

Royal Caribbean is waiving all rebooking fees and allowing affected passengers to select alternative itineraries across its fleet. **Is this cancellation covered by travel insurance?**
Most standard cruise travel insurance does not cover cruise line-initiated cancellations due to fleet redeployment—only passenger-initiated cancellations or event-based disruptions (weather, illness). Review your policy's fine print. Luxury travel insurance or cruise-specific plans may offer broader coverage; contact your insurer directly.

**Why is Freedom of the Seas being removed from 2027 schedules?**
