## Best Time to Visit Odisha

Odisha offers distinct travel experiences across seasons. **October to February** is the ideal period, with cooler temperatures (15-25°C), minimal rainfall, and perfect conditions for temple visits, beach exploration, and outdoor activities. **March-May** brings heat (30-40°C) but offers fewer crowds and lower accommodation rates. **June-September** is monsoon season, featuring lush green landscapes, fewer tourists, and special experiences like monsoon festivals, though travel requires weather flexibility. Peak season aligns with October-December, when accommodation prices peak but conditions are optimal.

## How to Get There

**By Air:** Fly into **Bhubaneswar (Biju Patnaik International Airport)**, Odisha's primary hub with connections from major Indian cities including Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Kolkata, and Hyderabad. International travelers can connect through Delhi or Mumbai. Flight duration from Delhi is approximately 2.5 hours; from Mumbai, 2 hours.

**By Rail:** Bhubaneswar Railway Station is well-connected to major Indian cities via express trains. Popular routes include Delhi-Bhubaneswar (24-36 hours), Mumbai-Bhubaneswar (30-36 hours), and Kolkata-Bhubaneswar (8-12 hours). The Rajdhani Express and Shatabdi Express provide premium options.

**By Road:** National Highway 5 (NH5) connects Odisha to Andhra Pradesh and Chennai, while NH16 links to West Bengal and Kolkata. Long-distance buses operate from neighboring states. Internal travel uses NH5 and NH16 to reach major destinations like Puri, Konark, and Chilika Lake.

**By Sea:** Port cities of Paradip and Visakhapatnam offer ferry connections; cruise tourism is emerging as an alternative arrival option for adventure travelers.

**Local Transport:** Taxis, auto-rickshaws, and bus networks operate throughout Odisha. Car rentals with drivers are recommended for multi-destination itineraries. Train networks connect major towns; local buses serve villages and temples.
