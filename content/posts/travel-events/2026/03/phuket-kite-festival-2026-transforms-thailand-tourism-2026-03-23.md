## Best Time to Visit

The **optimal window for the Phuket Kite Festival 2026 is late March through mid-April**. This period aligns with Thailand's Cool Season (November–April), offering ideal weather: daytime temperatures of 28–32°C (82–90°F), moderate humidity, and minimal rainfall. The festival spans multiple weekends, allowing flexibility:

- **Late March (March 21–30):** Fewer crowds, better accommodation rates, intimate festival experience.
- **Early April (April 1–15):** Larger festival activations, more vendor markets, but 20–30% higher accommodation prices.
- **Avoid:** Mid-April onward enters Thailand's Hot Season; rainfall increases and visitor comfort declines.

## How to Get There

**Flights:**
- **International**: Fly to **Phuket International Airport (HKT)** via major carriers (Thai Airways, Bangkok Airways, AirAsia). Hubs: Bangkok (2-hour domestic flight), Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, Hong Kong.
- **Regional airports**: Consider flying into **Krabi (HKT-adjacent, 90-minute drive to Phuket)** for alternative routes and potentially lower fares.

**Ground Transport:**
- **Phuket Airport to Festival Zones**: Taxis (`฿800–1,000` / $22–28), ride-sharing (Grab app, `฿600–800`), or pre-booked hotel transfers (`฿1,200–1,500`).
- **Inter-island travel**: Ferries and speedboats connect Phuket to nearby islands (Phi Phi, Similan, Phang Nga); most depart from Chalong Pier (30 min from airport).
- **Domestic travel**: Bus services link Phuket to Bangkok (14 hours), Krabi (3 hours), and Phang Nga (2 hours) via Thai companies (Nok Air, Greyhound).

**Visa Information:**
- **US, EU, Australian, Canadian citizens**: 60-day visa-exempt entry; or apply for 90-day Tourist Visa (TM.6) at Thai embassies.
- **Check current requirements** at the Royal Thai Embassy website before booking.
