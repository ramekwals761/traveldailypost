## Best Time to Visit

Kenya's **peak safari season** runs July to October, when wildlife congregates at water sources and the Great Migration dominates. However, **April to June** (long rains) offers lush landscapes, fewer tourists, and 40-50% lower accommodation costs—ideal for budget travelers and photographers. The **dry season from June to October** suits traditional safaris; **November to March** brings migration tail-end activity and warm coastal weather. The Holiday Tourism Fair (March 2026) happens during shoulder season—perfect for scouting deals for April-October travel.

## How to Get There

**By Air:** Fly into **Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA)** in Nairobi, Kenya's main hub. Direct flights from major European cities (London, Frankfurt) take 8-9 hours; US flights require 1-2 stops (typically via London or Frankfurt). Budget carriers like Kenya Airways, Ethiopian Airlines, and KLM offer competitive fares. Expect airfare in the range of `$600-1,200 USD` from North America and Europe.

**By Road:** From Nairobi (where Sarit Expo Centre is located), regional travel is straightforward. National parks and reserves are 2-6 hours by car or domestic flight. Nairobi to Masai Mara: 5 hours by road or 1 hour by charter flight. Standard 7-day safaris depart from Nairobi daily.

**Visa Requirements:** Most visitors need a **Kenyan eVisa** (obtainable online, `USD 50-100`, processed in 2-3 business days). EU and US citizens can also obtain visas on arrival. Check with Kenya's immigration website for current requirements.

**Ground Transport:** Hire a local safari operator (negotiated at the fair or via KATA members) for transportation. Budget: `KES 800-2,000` (~USD 6-15) per person per day for shared safari vans; private vehicles run `KES 3,000-5,000` (~USD 23-38) daily.
