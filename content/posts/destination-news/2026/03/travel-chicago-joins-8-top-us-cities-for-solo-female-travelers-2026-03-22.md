## What This Means for Travelers

- **Book now for spring/summer 2026:** Chicago hotel availability is tightening as solo female travelers adjust itineraries; reserve accommodations in walkable neighborhoods like Wicker Park, River North, and Lincoln Park early.

- **Use transit confidently:** The CTA (Chicago Transit Authority) has expanded night-lighting and emergency call systems on trains and buses. Download the `CTA App` before arrival for real-time safety updates and route planning.

- **Join female-focused tours:** Chicago now hosts 50+ women-centered walking tours, art experiences, and networking meetups specifically designed for solo female travelers. Book through platforms like `Airbnb Experiences` or local sites like `Choose Chicago`.

- **Explore high-rated safe neighborhoods:** Stay and explore **Pilsen** (street art, galleries), **Logan Square** (restaurants, nightlife), **Gold Coast** (shopping, museums), or **Near South Side** (cultural institutions). Avoid isolated areas after dark; use rideshare apps with verified driver profiles.

- **Access free safety resources:** The Chicago Convention and Visitors Bureau offers a **"Solo Female Traveler Guide"** with emergency contacts, women-owned businesses, and 24/7 support hotlines. Print or screenshot before departure.
