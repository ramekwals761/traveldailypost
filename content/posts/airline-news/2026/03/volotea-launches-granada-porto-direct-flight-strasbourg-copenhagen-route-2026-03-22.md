> **Volotea is reshaking Europe's regional aviation map in 2026.** The Spanish low-cost carrier just announced two major route expansions that promise to bypass major hubs and connect secondary cities directly. A new public service obligation (PSO) route between **Strasbourg and Copenhagen** launches alongside the airline's first-ever **Granada-to-Porto direct flight**, opening fresh travel corridors for leisure and business passengers across Southern and Northern Europe.

## What Happened: Context & Timeline

Volotea, Europe's leading airline for point-to-point regional travel, revealed its 2026 expansion strategy featuring two connectivity breakthroughs. The **Strasbourg (SXB) to Copenhagen (CPH)** route launches as a publicly subsidized service, meaning fares are regulated to ensure affordable access between the French Alsatian capital and Denmark's Nordic hub. Separately, the airline introduces its inaugural **Granada (GRX) to Porto (OPO)** direct flight, eliminating the need for connecting flights between Spain's Andalusian mountain city and Portugal's coastal metropolis.

Both routes respond to growing demand for secondary-city connections that skip expensive major hubs like Paris (`CDG`), Madrid (`MAD`), and Barcelona (`BCN`). PSO routes (public service obligations) are EU-regulated contracts requiring airlines to maintain routes that wouldn't be commercially viable without subsidy—ensuring connectivity to smaller airports.

The Granada-Porto route capitalizes on rising tourism and business travel between two increasingly popular destinations. Strasbourg's new Nordic connection strengthens ties between the EU capital and Scandinavian markets, particularly for business travelers avoiding expensive flag-carrier fares via larger airports.

## Industry Context & Analysis

Volotea's expansion mirrors a broader 2026 trend: regional airlines increasingly bypass mega-hubs. Traditional carriers (Lufthansa, Air France, SAS) historically funneled all traffic through Frankfurt, Paris, and Copenhagen, creating inefficient routing. Volotea and competitors like Ryanair are capturing market share by connecting secondary cities directly—what aviation analysts call "point-to-point democratization."

**What are my rights if the Granada-Porto or Strasbourg-Copenhagen flights are delayed or cancelled?**
EU Regulation 261/2004 applies to all flights departing EU airports. For delays exceeding 3 hours, you're entitled to €250-€600 compensation (depending on route distance) plus reimbursement for meals, accommodation, and rebooking. Volotea must offer rebooking on competitor flights at no extra cost. Document the delay with airport announcements and keep boarding passes. File claims at volotea.eu/passenger-rights within 6 months of the incident.
