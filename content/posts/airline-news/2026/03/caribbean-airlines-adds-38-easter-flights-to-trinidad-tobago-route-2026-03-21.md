## Frequently Asked Questions

**When should I book my Caribbean Tobago Easter flights?**
Ideally 8–10 weeks in advance (by late January for early April travel). The Caribbean Tobago Easter period sees inventory depletion by late February; prices spike dramatically in March. If you haven't booked yet, secure your seat this week—the 38 extra flights won't prevent sell-outs, they'll just extend the availability window by 1–2 weeks.

**What if my Caribbean Airlines flight is overbooked?**
Voluntary rebooking incentives are common during Easter. If your flight is oversold, Caribbean Airlines typically offers travel vouchers (`$50–$100 USD` value), meal vouchers, and confirmed seats on later flights. Families and passengers with connecting flights receive priority. Never accept an involuntary bump without compensation—you're entitled to this under IATA guidelines.

**Is flying Trinidad-Tobago cheaper than ferry?**
Fly time is 60 minutes; ferry is 5.5 hours. Air costs `$200–$320 roundtrip`; ferry is roughly `$60–$90 roundtrip`. The ferry is budget-friendly but slower and subject to weather cancellations during rough seas (common in April). For Easter week with time constraints, flying is worth the premium. Consider ferry only if you're flexible on dates and have 2+ days of travel time.
