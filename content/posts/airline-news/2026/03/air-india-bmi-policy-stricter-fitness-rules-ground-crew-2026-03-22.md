## Industry Context & Analysis

Air India's BMI policy arrives as global airlines face increasing scrutiny over crew standards. Unlike some Western carriers that have softened appearance-based requirements, Air India is moving in the opposite direction—tightening fitness benchmarks amid labor market tightness.

The timing is significant: India's aviation sector is experiencing rapid growth, with airports handling record passenger volumes. Airlines are competing fiercely for crew talent, making stricter policies potentially counterintuitive. However, Air India management likely views fitness standards as tied to aircraft safety, passenger service delivery, and uniform presentation—traditional pillars of international airline operations.

This policy could set a precedent across Indian carriers.
