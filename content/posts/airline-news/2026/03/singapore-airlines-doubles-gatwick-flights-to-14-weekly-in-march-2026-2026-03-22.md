## Singapore Airlines Expands London Gatwick to 14 Weekly Flights in March 2026

> **Singapore Airlines is doubling down on its London route.** Starting March 2026, the carrier will operate **14 weekly flights** between Singapore (SIN) and London Gatwick (LGW)—up from the current 10 weekly services. This expansion signals confidence in transatlantic demand and gives travelers significantly more booking options on one of Asia's most competitive international routes.

## What Happened: Context & Timeline

Singapore Airlines announced a major capacity increase on its Singapore-London Gatwick corridor, effective March 2026. The expansion adds four additional weekly flights to the route, representing a 40% boost in available seats. This move comes amid strong post-pandemic recovery in premium long-haul travel and increased competition from Middle Eastern carriers on the Asia-Europe axis.

The airline operates A350-900 aircraft on most Singapore-Gatwick services, offering a mix of First Class, Business Class, and Premium Economy cabins. The additional flights will improve schedule frequency for both leisure and business travelers, with multiple departure options throughout the week.

This is part of Singapore Airlines' broader network optimization strategy for 2026, which includes new routes and increased frequencies on high-demand corridors. The carrier has been aggressively competing against Emirates, Qatar Airways, and British Airways on UK-Asia routes.
