## What This Means for Travelers

- **Arrive 4+ hours early:** Standard "2 hours domestic" guidance is insufficient. Plan for 4-5 hour checkpoint delays at affected hubs, especially `IAH`, `DFW`, `ORD`, and `JFK`.

- **Monitor your flight in real-time:** Use FlightAware or your airline's app to track gate assignments, departure status, and potential rebooking offers every 15 minutes. Delays cascade quickly.

- **Call your airline NOW, don't just use the app:** Phone lines are overloaded, but reaching a human agent gives you priority rebooking on alternative flights, partner carriers, or next-day options before the system becomes fully saturated.

- **Request meal vouchers and hotel accommodations:** If you're rebooked on a flight 3+ hours later or overnight, you're entitled to food and lodging under DOT rules. Airlines are honoring these requests; ask before leaving the airport.

- **Book direct flights when possible:** If rebooking becomes necessary, prioritize non-stop or same-day itineraries to avoid cascading connection misses at secondary hubs like Dallas, Chicago, or Atlanta.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know if my flight at travel airport Houston or Dallas is affected?**
Visit FlightAware.com or your airline's app and search your flight number. Status shows as "Delayed," "Cancelled," or "On Time." Call your airline's main customer service line directly; the phone queue is long but faster than waiting for email confirmation. As of 3 PM local time, approximately 40-50% of flights in the 2-6 PM window were delayed or cancelled.

**Can I get a refund if my flight is cancelled today?**
Yes—under DOT regulations, cancelled flights entitle you to a full refund (airline credit or cash) or rebooking on the next available flight at no additional cost. Meals, hotels, and transportation to/from your rebooked flight are airline-provided if the new flight departs 3+ hours later. Document all expenses and keep receipts for reimbursement claims.

**Is TSA PreCheck or Clear worth it during delays like this?**
Absolutely. PreCheck users report 5-10 minute checkpoint times even during today's chaos, versus 3+ hours in standard lines. Clear (biometric scanning) cuts PreCheck times further to 2-3 minutes. At `$78-100/year` for PreCheck and `$189/year` for Clear, the ROI is immediate during crises like this. If you fly more than twice annually through major hubs, invest now.
