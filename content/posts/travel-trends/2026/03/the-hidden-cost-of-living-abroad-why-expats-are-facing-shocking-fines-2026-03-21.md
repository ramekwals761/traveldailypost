## The Insider's Perspective

- **Residency registration timing is critical:** Register within 30–90 days of arrival in most EU countries. Delay? Fines escalate exponentially. Pro tip: Ask your employer or housing agent to confirm the exact deadline—it varies by region and visa type.

- **Tax residency ≠ visa residency:** You can be a visa resident but not a tax resident (and vice versa). This confusion costs expats thousands. File your tax declaration immediately upon arrival, not at tax deadline time.

- **Keep a shadow records system:** Document everything—visa dates, property leases, tax filings, banking transactions. Digital backup saves lives (and legal cases). Authorities lose records; you won't.

- **Hire a compliance specialist before you move:** A 2-hour consultation (€150–€300) prevents €5,000+ in fines. It's insurance, not luxury.

- **Cross-border income requires immediate disclosure:** Freelancers, remote workers, and digital nomads are especially vulnerable. If you earn money in Country A but live in Country B, file both countries' taxes—don't guess about exemptions.

## Your Questions Answered

**What exactly makes an expat legally "tax resident" in their new country?**
Most countries use the **183-day rule**: spend more than half the year there, and you're a tax resident. But residence can also be determined by property ownership, family presence, or employment contracts. Some countries like Portugal use a points system. Read your destination's tax authority website or hire a professional—don't guess.

**If I'm working remotely for a US company while living in Europe, which country's taxes do I pay?**
Both, technically—but there are foreign earned income exclusions and tax treaties that prevent double taxation. A US expat typically files US taxes (you're always a US tax resident) and also files their country of residence's taxes. Tax credits usually balance it out, but only if filed correctly. Hire a US expat tax specialist; this is non-negotiable.

**How much should I budget for legal/tax compliance before moving?**
Minimum: €200–€400 for a consultation and initial setup. Annually: €500–€1,500 for ongoing compliance, depending on income complexity. Compare that to a €5,000+ fine—professional help pays for itself in two years.

**Can I appeal a fine if I claim ignorance?**
"I didn't know" is rarely a successful defense. Most tax and immigration authorities have published their rules in English online. Your best appeal argues procedural error or extenuating circumstances, not lack of knowledge. Document everything and appeal within 30–60 days.
